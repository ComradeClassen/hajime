# Hajime — Design System

*A single-file specification, extracted from the Hajime Style Bible (v1, April 26, 2026) and confirmed against the exercised hero artifacts. This document is the persistent reference for every Hajime project — pitch decks, in-game UI, marketing, build docs. It inherits nothing by default. The system is established here so that nothing downstream has to invent it.*

*Source of truth: `Hajime Style Bible.html`. Where the bible names something and the artifacts demonstrate it, the bible's framing is preserved verbatim and the artifact is referenced as the worked example. Where the artifacts use something the bible does not name, the section flags it as artifact-derived. Where the inputs disagree or leave a value unspecified, the question is parked at the end rather than fabricated.*

---

## 1. Identity and tone

Hajime is a deep coaching simulation about running a judo dojo across decades — a Football Manager for a sport that values restraint, where the central mechanic is the scarcity of the player's attention. The game's load-bearing architecture is a multi-decade worldgen of a real martial-arts ecosystem (New Jersey from 1960 forward, in 1.0); the dojo deep-dive sits inside that world, and the match engine sits inside the dojo. Three rings, one direction of dependency.

The visual and verbal grammar takes its cues from the sport, not the genre. Hajime reads as a sensei's notebook first, a sports broadcast second, a video game last. Surface is washi paper. Text is sumi ink. Tatami green is the institutional workhorse — calendar, schedule, the rooms where work happens. Vermilion is the score, the referee, the antagonist's escalation, and nothing else. Type is literary serif. The two referee calls — *Hajime* and *Matte* — are typographic events, not labels.

The system inherits nothing from generic indie-sim or sports-game UI conventions. No dark mode by default. No neon stat panels. No announcer voice. No drop shadows pretending to be depth. Hairlines, not borders. Italic asides, not callouts. One stamp of color, not three. The discipline is what makes the system feel like one thing across a multi-year build.

The reader of any Hajime surface should feel like a knowledgeable friend has just sat down with them and walked them carefully through what is being built.

## 2. Palette

Eight colors. Each has a job. The vermilion is the only loud one — and it stays rare on purpose, the way a referee's flag stays rare. Whites and blacks are warm, paper-toned, never sterile.

| Name | Hex | OKLCH | Role | Use restriction |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Washi · 和紙 | `#F3EDE0` | `oklch(.93 .017 84)` | Primary surface | The page. The mat. Default everything that is not text. |
| Washi-2 · 和紙弐 | `#E8E0CD` | *not specified* | Recessed surface | Cards, panels, the hierarchy of "this is contained." |
| Sumi · 墨 | `#15140F` | `oklch(.18 .01 80)` | Primary text and full-strength rules | All primary text. Hairline rules at full strength. The voice of the document. |
| Tatami · 畳 | `#6F7A4A` | *not specified* | Institutional workhorse | The dojo loop, calendar, schedule. Where work gets done. |
| Tatami-deep · 深畳 | `#4D5634` | *not specified* | Headers on tatami panels, charts, long shadows | Used on tatami fields, never as a primary surface. |
| Shu · 朱 | `#B8331F` | `oklch(.51 .163 33)` | Vermilion — referee, score, the stamp | **Less than 5% of any view.** Match score state, referee actions, the antagonist's escalation, error states, the cover-stamp. Never as a section background. Never as a button fill on more than one button per view. |
| Shinchu · 真鍮 | `#B08A3A` | *not specified* | Aged brass — lineage moments | Belt promotions, the founding lineage seal, championship medals, the succession event. **Even rarer than vermilion.** If a surface has no lineage moment, brass does not appear. |
| Rule · 罫 | `#C4B896` | *not specified* | Hairline rules, dividers, table grid | Quiet structure. Never as a fill. |

*Plus the shadow token the bible names alongside the palette: `0 1px 0 rgba(21,20,15,.05), 0 12px 32px -16px rgba(21,20,15,.18)` — used on the deepest cards and the artifact frame.*

### Why this palette

**No pure black. No pure white. By intent.** Sumi is warm-tinted near-black; Washi is the color of unbleached paper. *The result is a document that reads as made by a human in a real room, not generated by software in the dark.*

**Tatami green is the institutional voice — not the loud one.** The dojo's identity color is green because tatami is green, because the calmest part of the room is the most important part. *Football Manager uses purple for the same reason — calendar, work, structure.*

**Vermilion is the score, and only that.** Shu appears at: match score state, referee actions, the antagonist's escalation, error states, the cover-stamp. *Loud color anywhere but the score is a violation of the system — see Don't 04.*

**Brass is the lineage, rarer still.** Shinchu appears at: belt promotions, the founding lineage seal, championship medals, the succession event. *If the deck has no slide about lineage, brass does not appear in the deck.*

## 3. Typography

Three workhorse faces and one accent. Every type token in the system resolves to one of these four. The forbidden list is load-bearing: it carries the visual language that the system is defined against.

**Display — Fraunces, weights 300–600, optical size 144.** Slide titles, section heads, the masthead, every L1 and L2 in the hierarchy. Italic for specific words inside titles — *twins, hajime, sensei.* Letter-spacing -0.02em at display sizes; -0.015em at section sizes. Never used below 22px. *Variable optical sizes mean one family handles 96px masthead and 18px section kicker without a second face. Soft humanist contrast — no Didone glamour, no slab toughness. Reads as literary, not corporate.*

**Body — Source Serif 4, weights 400–600, optical size 14.** Paragraph text, captions, slide bodies, the L4 register. Generous leading (1.5–1.55). Pretty text-wrap on. Max 64ch line length. *Pairs with Fraunces' display voice without competing with it.*

**Data and UI — JetBrains Mono, weights 400–500.** Match logs, stat panels, calendar grid, build-status tags, every L5 eyebrow label. Letter-spacing 0.14–0.22em uppercase for all-caps labels. *The mono carries every numeric, label, and tag in the system; a serif would not hold the grid.*

**Accent — Shippori Mincho, weights 400–700.** Reserved for actual Japanese characters: 始め on the cover, 道場 in section dividers, calligraphy plates over photo. **Never set Latin in Mincho in this system.** *Mincho's Latin glyphs are pleasant but signal "Japan-themed product" rather than serious work. Used as accent only.*

### Optical-size discipline

The bible commits to specific optical sizes by context, which Fraunces honors via its variable axis. L1 titles use opsz 144; L2 sections use opsz 96; the masthead in the bible itself sets `font-variation-settings: "opsz" 144` at 96px display sizes. *Optical-size discipline is what keeps a single family literate at five different scales.*

### Forbidden — do not use

**Inter for headlines. Roboto anywhere. Arial. Helvetica Neue. system-ui. Montserrat. Poppins.** *They are the visual language of the old deck and every other indie deck. Hajime is not them. This list is enforced; if a surface needs a face that is not in the four above, the answer is to use one of the four above differently, not to add a fifth.*

## 4. Hierarchy

Five primary levels and two specialized registers. Every surface in the system — every slide of every deck, every screen of the eventual game — uses these and nothing else. *The hierarchy is the design system.*

| Level | Tag | Face and weight | Size | Leading | Color | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| L1 | TITLE | Fraunces 500, opsz 144, letter-spacing -0.02em | 56–96px | 0.92–1.0 | Sumi on Washi | Slide titles, masthead, the cover. |
| L2 | SECTION | Fraunces 500, opsz 96, letter-spacing -0.015em | 32–42px | 1.05 | Sumi | Section heads inside a deck or a screen. |
| L3 | SUB | Fraunces italic 600 | 22px | — | Sumi-2 | Used as kicker or pull-quote, never as a standalone heading. |
| L4 | BODY | Source Serif 4 400 | 16–18px | 1.5–1.55 | Sumi-2, max 64ch | Paragraph text, captions, slide bodies. |
| L5 | LABEL | JetBrains Mono 500, letter-spacing 0.14–0.22em | 10–12px | — | Shu-deep on Washi | All-caps eyebrows, system tags, ring/phase markers. |
| L+ | PROSE | Source Serif italic 400 | 15–17px | 1.6 | Sumi | The in-game match prose register. |
| L+ | DATA | JetBrains Mono 400, tabular nums | 13px | — | Sumi | Match panel and roster grid. |

*Note: the bible specifies "Sumi-2" as a CSS variable (`#2A261D`) but does not include it in the palette swatch list. It is used at L3 and L4 as a softer text color and inside the Matte typographic event as the kanji color. Treat as a recognized stop of Sumi rather than a separate palette entry.*

### How the levels compose

The bible spells the surface composition out as five typographic moves a deck (or a screen) makes in order. They are reproduced here because they apply to every Hajime surface, not just slides:

**01 · OPEN — Cover.** L1 title, L5 label, the *始め* stamp. No body copy.
**02 · SET — Section.** L2 + L3 + one block of L4. White space wins.
**03 · SHOW — Anchoring scene.** L+ prose + L+ data side-by-side. The system speaking.
**04 · TELL — Argument.** L2 + L4. One claim, supported by one paragraph. Stop.
**05 · CLOSE — Ask.** L1, usually one sentence, over Washi-2. Stamp.

*This is the rhythm. Surfaces that omit one of these moves rarely fail; surfaces that try to do all five inside a single block always do.*

## 5. The two calls — *始め* and *待て*

Hajime and Matte are not labels in this system. They are typographic events. The whole interface — game, deck, marketing — bends around them. They are the system's two largest objects, and they only ever appear at the moments they would actually be called. *Begin a thing. Suspend a thing. Nothing else.*

### Hajime · 始め — the beginning

| Property | Spec |
|---|---|
| Scale | 180–280px display. Larger than any L1 title in the system. Always the largest object on its surface. *The Matte Event Card hero artifact pushes this to 300px on a 1920px-wide canvas — read the upper bound as "as large as the surface allows."* |
| Face | Shippori Mincho 700 for 始め · Fraunces italic 300 for the romaji. Set together, never separately. |
| Color | Sumi for the kanji, Shu for the romaji. *The only place in the system where the two colors touch each other directly.* |
| Motion | In-game: snaps in, holds 800ms, decays to a corner timestamp. In static media: full-bleed, alone, no caption above it. |
| Demands | Attention. The player's eye is taken. The next thing they see is consequential. |
| Frequency | Once per round, once per session intro, once on the deck cover, once on the press kit. **Always feels like the first thing.** |

### Matte · 待て — the pause

| Property | Spec |
|---|---|
| Scale | Same as Hajime. Never smaller. Same display rectangle. *The pair is symmetric.* |
| Face | Shippori Mincho 500 (lighter weight than Hajime) · Fraunces italic 300 in tatami-deep. |
| Color | Sumi-2 (softer) on Washi-2 (recessed). *The release is in the contrast drop, not in a different palette.* |
| Motion | Decelerates in. Holds longer than Hajime — 1.4–2 seconds. The player is meant to sit with it. Then the coach window opens beneath. |
| Permits | Rest. The next-thing-to-do is now a choice, not a reaction. Instructions can be issued. Conversations can begin. |
| Frequency | Many times per match. Many times per session. Used as the deck's "section break" punctuation between Hajime-weight ideas. |

### The pair, not the calls

Hajime is never used without Matte being part of the system. The deck cover is Hajime; the closing slide is Matte. The press kit's first image is Hajime; the last sentence is Matte. *They are parentheses around everything Hajime makes.*

### Never decoration. Always punctuation.

If a surface uses Hajime as a graphic flourish — a watermark, a pattern, a corner ornament — the system has been violated. **The call only ever appears at the moment it would actually be called.** Begin a thing. Suspend a thing. Nothing else. *The pre-Style-Bible Pitch Deck v1 set 始 at 1100px as a decorative background watermark on the cover. This is the canonical violation; do not repeat it.*

## 6. Components

Surfaces named by the Style Bible or recurring across two or more hero artifacts. Specs reference the artifact that demonstrates the component.

### The Stamp

A vermilion square, 38–64px, holding a single Japanese character — typically 道 (dojo), 待 (matte), 始 (hajime), or a discipline emblem. Shippori Mincho 500–700 in Washi cream, set on Shu fill, with a 1.5–2px inset cream rule and a 1px Shu-deep outer rule. Rotated -3°. *A signature mark, not a logo. Appears once per surface, never as repeated decoration.* Specified in the Style Bible masthead and the Watched Randori topbar.

### The Mission Block

A Washi-2 panel with a 4px Shu left edge and a 1px Rule border. L4 display-weight prose at 26px inside. A Shu-deep mono corner tag in the upper right. *The shape a single load-bearing claim takes when it cannot be carried by an L1 alone.* Used in the bible's "Mission for the deck" section.

### The Type Card and the Rule Card

A 1px Rule border, Washi-2 fill, mono eyebrow at the top, L3 weight title, L4 body, optional do-line at the bottom (two columns: green DO, Shu DON'T, both in mono). *The chassis for principles, rules, and rationale — the recurring small unit of explanation across the bible.*

### The Hierarchy Row

A grid row inside a Rule-bordered Washi-2 surface: an L5 mono tag on the left, the type sample in the middle, a mono spec column on the right. Alternating rows use a translucent white tint. *The shape "here is the spec, here is the sample, here is the use" takes when it appears as a list.*

### The Voice Card

A Washi-2 panel with a 1px Rule border. L5 mono label, L3 name, then a sample set in that voice's actual register. *The shape voice documentation takes when shown side-by-side. Used in the bible's "Three voices" section and replicated in the Watched Randori footer.*

### The Mat Stage

A two-layer rendering of the tatami: a green outer field (`tatami-mat #8A9568`) with a 14px Shu safety border, then a darker green inner field (`tatami #6F7A4A`) with a 28-grid hairline pattern at `rgba(21,20,15,.04)`. Two figure clip-paths centered, with a grip-graph SVG overlay above them. *The match's actual surface, not a UI representation of it.* Demonstrated in Watched Randori. Note the safety border is the only place in the system where Shu functions as a background fill — a deliberate exception that cites the real referee zone, not a UI choice.

### The Match Prose Stream

A Washi panel containing timed lines — left column a 56px mono timestamp at L+ DATA spec, right column the prose at L+ PROSE spec. Lines have implicit subtypes: beat (italic Sumi-2), event (Sumi with bold key term), cue (Shu-deep with a leading red arrow), silent (55% opacity), and live (current tick, with a Shu pulse). *The match log register made spatial.* Demonstrated in Watched Randori.

### The Pair Card

A Washi card with 1px Rule border. Top: a dotted-bottom L5 mono row carrying weight class, meeting count (1ST, 2ND ⟲ for rematch), and a Shu pulse if a flagged event just resolved. Middle: two judoka names in Fraunces 500 with an L5 × separator, then a belt-color row using a constrained subset of belt hex tokens. Bottom: a dashed-top trace of the most recent two simulation events in mono L+. A vermilion-ruled "FOCUS" tag pinned at the bottom right. *The shape a peripheral pair takes — high information density, low attention cost.* Demonstrated in Watched Randori.

### The Chair (data panel)

A right-column Washi surface, ~340px wide, holding the watched judoka's biographical line, a stats grid (mono key, hairline bar, mono value with up/down arrows in green/red), an embedded grip-graph render, an instructions tray of lettered options, and an attention-budget block at the bottom. *The player's seat. Always on the right. Always the densest surface on the screen, and always quiet about it.* Demonstrated in both Watched Randori and the Matte Event Card.

### The Coach-Strip

A Washi-2 horizontal block that appears beneath a paused match. Three-column: the Matte typographic event on the left (the smaller "in-context" treatment, 50px kanji), a Rule-divided italic context line in the middle, and three buttons on the right — two outlined Washi buttons and one filled-Sumi primary, all keyboard-prefixed in mono with a Shu key indicator. *The shape the coach window takes when the chair is already on screen. Subordinate to the full Matte event card, dominant over everything else in the panel.*

### The Stat Cell strip

A 3-cell horizontal display, 1px Rule border, Washi fills, internal Rule dividers. Each cell: a Shu-deep mono label at the top, a Fraunces 500 value at 28–34px below it, with optional small italic qualifiers in Sumi-3. *The shape a moment-in-time scoreboard takes — round time, score, stamina. Not a stat panel. A scoreboard.* Demonstrated on the Matte Event Card.

### The Action Row

A full-width row inside the chair, 1px Rule border, Washi-2 fill (the primary action gets a 1.5px Sumi border and a Washi fill instead). Three columns: a 36px mono key indicator in a small bordered square, the action body in Source Serif 4 with bold key terms in Sumi and italic consequences in Shu-deep, and a mono arrow on the right. A "notice" variant uses a dashed border and an italic Sumi-3 body for non-actions ("say nothing"). *The shape a coach decision takes — never a button, always a sentence.* Demonstrated on the Matte Event Card.

### The Attention Budget

A Sumi block (text reverses to Washi) inside the chair, ~12px padding. Mono header in Washi-3, Fraunces 500 numeric value at 34px with the denominator italicized in Shu, an italic Source Serif consequence note on the right. *The only surface in the system that inverts to dark — and only because attention is the resource the player is spending, not the resource the dojo is on.* Demonstrated in Watched Randori.

### The Frame Footer

A four-column Washi-2 strip across the bottom of an artifact. Each column: bold mono L5 label, plain mono body, with one italic Source Serif aside permitted. *The signature row that pins an artifact to its place in the system — Ring, Phase, Principle, Voice, or whatever the artifact most needs to declare.* Demonstrated in Watched Randori and the Matte Event Card.

### The Broadcast Strip

A top-of-surface row that names the match's official context — show name in mono L5, location in Sumi-3 mono, timestamp on the right. Used only on the official-feed framing of a match (e.g., the Matte Event Card, which is set at the Rheinland U21 Open 2027). *The "what room are we in" line. Carries no game-data; carries the world the data is happening inside.*

### The Note Row

A Washi-2 strip with an italic Source Serif 13px body in Sumi-3, prefixed with a Shu-deep `NOTE ·` mono label in normal weight. *The editorial caveat that makes a surface honest about its constraints. Used sparingly — once per artifact at most.* Demonstrated on the Matte Event Card.

## 7. Voice

Three named voices, drawn from the master document's tone rules and confirmed by the bible. A fourth voice (Legends / archival) is named in the master doc post-worldgen-reframe but does not yet appear in the bible's voice grid; see Open Questions.

### Match log — knowledgeable sportswriter

Specific. Calm. Loves the sport. Doesn't explain what kuzushi is — assumes the reader is paying attention or willing to learn. Neil Adams commentary register: quiet, diagnostic, annotating deltas. *Physics resolves; prose marks. The simulation never announces the physics.*

> 0:18  Tanaka attempts seoi-nage —
>       → Sato sprawls. Hips back. Throw stuffed.
>       → Slight scramble. Ne-waza window: 11%.
>       → Both stand. Grips reset.

### Coach window — intimate, slightly worried

The player's view of their fighter. Quiet, focused, slightly worried. Physics-aware without being clinical — *"His entry window is closing"*, not *"moment arm modifier is below threshold."*

> "He went for his strongest throw too early. Sato read it. The grip fight is going against him on the right side."

### Dojo prose — Sunday evening, the notepad

Warm. The texture of routine — sweat, repetition, small jokes, the smell of the mats. Weekly roundups feel like the sensei sitting down on a Sunday evening with the notepad. Margin notes feel like a real sensei's real notes, not like a game surfacing information.

> Marco showed up early again. He has been quiet about the tournament — three conversations in and I still don't know if he wants to go. His grip is improving but only against the lighter partners.

### Everywhere

No hype. No announcer voice. *Hajime is not the UFC. It is judo — a sport with deep roots, formal etiquette, and an understated culture.* Every fighter is treated with dignity. Including the opponents. Including the ones who lose. Especially the ones who lose.

## 8. Don'ts

The four don'ts the Style Bible names explicitly so they cannot sneak back in, plus the additional don'ts that surface across the hero artifacts.

### From the Style Bible

**01. Dark mode by default.** Hajime's surface is paper. Dark backgrounds belong to the in-game match overlay only — not slides, not the website, not the press kit. *The deck is a document, not a console UI.* DO: Washi `#F3EDE0` surface, Sumi text. DON'T: `#0E0E12` background with neon stat bars.

**02. Stock-game iconography.** No swords, no dojo gates rendered in SVG, no katana silhouettes, no Japanese-flag motifs. The lineage is in the type and the stamp. *Imagery is photographic, archival, or absent.* DO: black-and-white archival photo, full-bleed, with caption. DON'T: SVG katana crossing a torii gate.

**03. Sim-stat decoration.** Hajime is a serious sim. The deck is not. *Stat panels appear only where they are showing the actual game — never as decorative trim around a marketing claim.* DO: one real stat block, captioned as a screen. DON'T: three radar charts of made-up numbers.

**04. Loud color anywhere but the score.** Vermilion only on the things vermilion is for. Tatami green is the workhorse. *If a slide needs three colors to communicate, the slide needs less content, not more color.* DO: one stamp of Shu per spread, max. DON'T: gradient backgrounds. Ever.

### Surfaced from the artifacts

**05. The kanji is never decoration.** The pre-Style-Bible Pitch Deck v1 set 始 at 1100px as a decorative watermark on the cover slide and 900px on the close slide. This is the canonical violation. *The kanji is a typographic event when it is the call, and a stamp when it is the mark — never wallpaper.*

**06. No infographic chrome around the simulation.** Pitch Deck v1 wrapped its event card in concentric box-shadow ring frames (`0 0 0 1px ... 0 0 0 4px ... 0 0 0 5px ...`) to simulate depth and weight. The system's event surface — see the Matte Event Card hero artifact — uses a single 1px Rule border and a 6px Tatami left edge. *Depth is in the type and the negative space, not in the chrome.*

**07. Amber glow is not the system's gold.** Pitch Deck v1 used "amber-bulb" yellow as the primary accent — a warm glow color that reads as marketing. The system's gold is Shinchu brass, used only for lineage. *If a surface is glowing, it is wrong. The system does not glow.*

**08. No 5-tier gradient progression bars.** Pitch Deck v1 rendered the local-to-Olympic pipeline as a five-card row with a horizontal gradient line and glowing dots. *The pipeline is real, but it is not an infographic. When the system needs to show progression, it shows it as a list of L4 paragraphs separated by hairlines, not as a marketing diagram.*

## 9. Open Questions

Items where the inputs disagree, items the inputs leave unspecified that Claude Design will need a decision on, items where the inputs reference materials that were not present in the workspace, and items where this extraction made a judgment call worth flagging.

### Inputs not present in the workspace

The brief specifies two Tier 2 hero artifacts that should ground the worldgen and worldgen-sequence visuals: `nj-worldgen-1996.html` (the Ring 2 worldgen surface, with the NJ map, four discipline icons, cultural inflection text, sprite sheet, chronicle log) and `worldgen-sequence.html` (the cold-open newspaper-clipping surface plus decade ticks 1960/1970/1980/1990, the era marker system, the event log register, the named-ages structure). Neither file was present at extraction time. The Ring 2 spec (`ring-2-worldgen-spec-v2.md`) commits to all the visual elements those artifacts would have demonstrated — four discipline icons (Kodokan emblem for judo, Gracie triangle for BJJ, crossed gloves for boxing, wrestlers grappling for wrestling); a 2D New Jersey map with period-appropriate styling that modernizes subtly across decades; procgen portraits for every chronicle-active judoka; group photographs as a chronicle entry type rendered onto dojo walls; visual Easter eggs in the daily layer (cohort members bowing to a deceased member's photo); 64×64 px sprites for the icon families — but the Style Bible does not yet name any of them. **These are open commitments, not open specs.** The system needs the worldgen artifacts (or the bible's next revision) to lock the spec.

### Discrepancies between the bible and the artifacts

**The "six colors" framing.** The Style Bible's section 03 kicker says "Six colors. Each with a job." The palette grid in the same section lists eight swatches. This document treats eight as authoritative because the swatches are the artifact, the kicker is the framing — and because Washi-2 and Tatami-deep are independently named with use restrictions. Worth a clarifying revision in the bible.

**The Sumi-2 and Sumi-3 stops.** Sumi-2 (`#2A261D`) appears in the bible's CSS variables and is used at L3, L4, and inside the Matte event itself, but is not in the swatch list. Sumi-3 (`#4A4538`) appears only in the artifacts (Watched Randori, Matte Event Card) — not in the bible at all — and is used as the soft-meta text color throughout. Treat both as recognized stops of Sumi for now; the bible should either promote them into the palette or rule them out.

**Tatami-mat (`#8A9568`).** Used in Watched Randori as the actual mat surface, distinct from Tatami (`#6F7A4A`) which sits inside it as the inner field. Not in the bible. *Probably a deliberate choice for the rendered mat, not a system color — but it should be either named or ruled out.*

**Washi-3 (`#D8CFB8`).** Appears in both artifacts' CSS variable blocks but not in the bible. Used as the deepest paper tone. Same status as Sumi-3 — recognized in artifact CSS, unnamed in the bible.

**Belt color tokens.** The Pair Card in Watched Randori uses six belt-color tokens (yellow `#A48727`, orange `#9D5D20`, green `#3B6B34`, brown `#5A3A1F`, black, white). These are not part of the eight-color system palette and should not be — they are sport tokens, like the safety-border red on the mat. Worth declaring as a separate, named "belt palette" that is exempt from the system rules.

### Voice gaps

**A fourth voice — Legends / archival.** The master document's TONE RULES section, updated May 4, 2026 with the worldgen reframe, names a new voice that the bible's three-voice grammar does not yet account for: "calm, distant, and complete. Past tense. No drama in the writing — drama is in the reader's recognition that *this happened.*" Sample: *"Yamada won the 1974 state final on a contested decision over the favorite. He retired the following year and never coached. His sensei was Yonezuka of Cranford."* This is the voice the chronicle and legends-mode browse will use. **The bible should add this as a fourth voice card before any worldgen surface ships.**

### OKLCH coverage

The bible gives OKLCH values for three of the eight palette colors (Washi, Sumi, Shu) and hex-only for the other five (Washi-2, Tatami, Tatami-deep, Shinchu, Rule). Claude Design works in OKLCH for theme generation; the missing five should be computed and committed before the next round of system work.

### Specifications the bible does not cover

The Style Bible v1 is comprehensive on color, type, hierarchy, voice, the two calls, and the Don'ts. It does not specify, and Claude Design will plausibly ask:

**Spacing tokens.** The bible and artifacts use values like 64px, 88px, 28px, 14px, 22px without a token system. *A scale exists in the artifacts (the page padding is 88×64, section margin-top is 88, sec-head gap is 28), but it is not declared.*

**Shadow values.** One shadow is named in the bible (`0 1px 0 rgba(21,20,15,.05), 0 12px 32px -16px rgba(21,20,15,.18)`) and used on the artifact frame. No other elevation tokens exist.

**Animation timings.** The two-call event spec names two durations — Hajime holds 800ms, Matte holds 1.4–2 seconds — and one easing direction (Matte decelerates in). Nothing else in the system specifies motion. The bible's principle ("hairlines, not borders; italic asides, not callouts") implies motion should be similarly restrained, but no tokens exist.

**Responsive breakpoints.** The artifacts are sized for fixed canvases (1180px wide for the bible, 1520px for Watched Randori, 1920×1080 for the Matte Event Card). The system has no stated mobile, tablet, or arbitrary-viewport guidance.
